CountingArithmetic

Counting is arguably one of the most fundamental operations in Mathematics.
The ubiquity of this operation is encapsulated best by the idea of the integers-- anything there is to count may be labeled by integers.
So, how do we go about doing it?
The simplest possible count is nothing.
Some everyday examples of this include: a box with its contents removed, an page before it has been written, or a college student's savings account.
For convenience, we give this quantity a name and symbol: 0 (zero)
